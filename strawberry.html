1. Technically, it is an aggregate accessory fruit, meaning that the fleshy part is derived not from the plant's ovaries but from the receptacle that holds the ovaries.
  2. Strawberries are the first fruit to ripen each spring.
  3. There are 200 seeds on an average strawberry.
  4. The seeds can grow into new strawberry plants, but most instead reproduce through runners.
  5. They're an excellent source of vitamin C and manganese and also contain decent amounts of folate (vitamin B9) and potassium.
  6. Strawberries are very rich in antioxidants and plant compounds, which may have benefits for heart health and blood sugar control ( 1 , 2 ).
  7. Strawberries are believed to help reduce the risk of heart disease and certain cancers.
